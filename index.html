About Adhira Trust

A Public Charitable Trust with 80 G exemption, committed to serving humanity, conserving the environment and extending charity and spiritual sevas specifically annadhanam (food donation) medical assistance and education support.


Adhira Trust is a Public Charitable Trust formed in 2024 with 80 G exemption. The Trust is driven by the principles of human compassion and duty to serve the society at large and protect the environment as a moral and social responsibility.


Our Core Objectives

  • Medical support to the underprivileged
  • Sponsoring education to break poverty cycles
  • Annadanam to the deprived and needy
  • Protection and welfare of indigenous cattle breeds by supporting goshalas with fodder
  • Conservation of the ecosystem through green belt projects, timber tree plantations, and protection of temple lands from encroachment

Temple Land Reclamation & Green Belt Project

Adhira Trust has taken up a pilot project to maintain and support reclaimed fragmented and unproductive temple punjai lands spread over an extent of 30000.sq ft.In order to make them productive and generate revenue sources for the upkeep proper fencing, gates for securing the area from encroachment and borewells have been provided for watering 1000 timber tree saplings planted so far. This initiative can be further expanded with your donation towards installation of solar panels for watering the saplings this summer through drip irrigation.

The reclaimed land has been made productive by careful measurement, weeding, fencing, gating, borewell installation, and planting of timber tree saplings as a pilot project.

This initiative not only protects temple assets but also creates sustainable income and prevents future encroachments.

Our Ongoing Services

  • Annadanam in specially-abled orphanage homes
  • Annadanam in temples and pathashalas
  • Regular fodder support for goshalas
  • Educational support for underprivileged students

One underprivileged undergraduate student has been supported with semester fees and a part-time job opportunity. Such support has been made possible only through generous donors.
